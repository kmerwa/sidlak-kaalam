Lesson 3: Processes and Landforms Along Plate Boundaries

Power Up Your Skill!

Use your knowledge of the different processes occurring at plate boundaries to solve the following geologic cases.

Case 1:

You observe a deep ocean trench, strong earthquakes, and a chain of volcanoes on the edge of a continent. What process is occurring here?

Case 2:

A mid-ocean ridge is forming where two oceanic plates move away from each other. What process is happening?

Case 3:

Two continental plates collide, forming high mountain ranges like the Himalayas. What process is taking place?

Case 4:

You observe shallow earthquakes along a region like the San Andreas Fault with no volcanic activity. What type of plate interaction is this?

Case 5:

An oceanic plate is subducting beneath another oceanic plate, forming an island arc. What is this process called?

Case 6:

A rift valley is forming in a continental region. What process is responsible for this landform?

Case 7:

New crust is continuously created as magma rises between diverging plates. What process does this describe?

Case 8:

A trench and volcanic arc are found parallel to each other in an oceanic-continental setting. What is the dominant process?

Case 9:

You discover a region with significant uplift and mountain formation but no volcanic activity. It's far from any ocean. What is happening?

Case 10:

Along a transform boundary, which process is most common?

Case 11:

A new volcanic island forms in a curved chain over a subduction zone. What is the likely setting?

Case 12:

Plates are pulled into the mantle by the weight of a descending slab. What process drives this?

Case 13:

Molten rock rises between plates at a mid-ocean ridge. What is this process called?

Case 14:

A series of earthquakes occur where two plates grind past each other without producing volcanoes. What process is responsible?

Case 15:

A continent splits apart, creating a rift system like in East Africa. What boundary process is this?
